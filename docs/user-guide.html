User Guide

Everything you need to use DietReport — from first launch to reading your weekly stats.

01. First Launch & Onboarding

When you open DietReport for the first time, the app sends you straight to Settings to complete your daily schedule. There is no account required — everything stays on your device.

Fill in your schedule, tap Save, and reminders start immediately. Every subsequent launch goes straight to Home.

02. Home Screen

Your daily dashboard. Every meal reminder slot for today is listed here with its current status.

Slot cards

StatusWhat it means
⏳ Pending The 30-minute logging window is open — tap to log now
✓ Success You logged a meal photo within the window
✕ Failed The window expired, or you skipped the lock screen early

Next-meal banner

A banner at the top of the list shows the time of the next upcoming reminder, so you always know when to expect the next prompt.

Extra meal button

Tap + Log extra meal to log a meal outside a scheduled slot — useful for unplanned snacks or off-schedule eating.

Permission warning banner

If the exact-alarm or notification permission is revoked, a yellow warning banner appears at the top of the screen. Tap Dismiss to hide it temporarily; re-granting the permission in system settings clears it permanently.

03. Logging a Meal

The Meal Logging screen opens when you tap a ⏳ Pending slot or the Log meal action in a reminder notification.

Taking a photo

  1. Grant camera permission when prompted (one-time only).
  2. The camera preview fills the screen.
  3. Tap Capture to take a photo with the rear camera.
  4. Review the photo. Tap Retake to reshoot, or Confirm to save.

Picking from gallery

Tap Gallery at any point to open the system image picker and choose an existing photo instead of taking a new one. Useful if you already photographed your meal with the regular camera app.

The 30-minute window

⏱

A log counts as Success only if you tap Confirm within 30 minutes of the scheduled reminder time. After that window, the slot is automatically marked Failed — regardless of whether you logged a photo.

04. Focus Lock Screen

After confirming a meal log, the app enters a 10-minute focus period. The idea: eat without your phone.

  • Fullscreen. System bars are hidden for the duration.
  • Screen stays on for the entire 10-minute countdown.
  • Timer reaches zero → lock ends, app returns to Home, meal stays ✓ Success.

Ending the lock early

Tap Unlock (mark as failed) or press the system Back button to cancel. This marks the meal as ✕ Failed. The same thing happens if you background the app — press Home, switch apps, or pull down the notification shade.

—

This is intentional. The lock screen only benefits you if you actually stay on it.

05. Photo Gallery

The Photos tab shows a scrollable grid of every meal photo you have logged, sorted with the most recent first.

  • Tap any photo to view it full-size.
  • Photos are stored locally and are never uploaded anywhere.
  • Uninstalling the app removes all photos.

06. Settings

Reached at any time via the Settings tab in the bottom navigation bar.

How reminders are spaced: Starting at wake time + delay, a reminder fires every 3 hours until 2 hours before bedtime.
Example: wake 07:00, delay 30 min, bedtime 23:00 → reminders at 07:30 · 10:30 · 13:30 · 16:30 · 19:30.

Notification Sound

OptionDescription
DefaultSystem default notification tone

Activity Monitor

When enabled, the app reads your daily phone-usage patterns to automatically infer wake and bedtime, overriding the manual fields. Requires Usage Access permission — grant it in System Settings → Privacy → Usage Access.

Saving

Tap Save to validate and apply all values. A green confirmation card appears briefly on success. The reminder schedule is rebuilt immediately — no restart needed.

07. Report

View your meal-logging statistics via the Report tab. Toggle between Weekly (last 7 days) and Monthly (last 30 days).

SectionWhat it shows

By HourSuccess rate grouped by the scheduled reminder hour
Time of DayMorning (before 12:00) · Afternoon (12–17) · Evening (after 17:00)

Tap Share to generate a plain-text summary and open the Android Sharesheet. A clear-data action is also available to reset your history.

—

A meal counts as Success only if it was logged and the lock screen countdown completed. Exiting the lock screen early marks it Failed.

08. Notifications & Reminders

The app uses AlarmManager exact alarms — not vague background jobs — so reminders fire at the precise scheduled time, even in low-power mode (subject to the exact-alarm permission).

Notification actions

ActionWhat happens
Log mealOpens the Meal Logging screen for that slot directly
SnoozeReschedules the reminder 10 minutes later

After a device reboot

The reminder schedule is automatically restored by a boot receiver. You do not need to open the app after restarting your phone.

After a timezone change

A receiver detects the change and rebuilds the schedule using your new local time. Crossing a timezone while travelling just works.

Exact-alarm permission

⚠

If you deny exact alarms, reminders may fire several minutes late due to Android battery-optimization batching. A warning banner appears on Home.

To re-grant: Settings → Apps → DietReport → Permissions → Alarms & reminders → Allow.

09. Tips & Best Practices

  • Grant exact alarms. Late reminders defeat the purpose of timed tracking.
  • Use the Gallery option. If you already photographed your meal with the regular camera app, pick the existing photo — no retake needed.
  • Stay on the Lock screen. Backgrounding the app cancels the countdown and marks the meal Failed.
  • Do not clear app data. This erases your entire meal history and all settings.
  • Keep notifications enabled for the DietReport channel in system settings, or you will not receive reminders.
  • Log extra meals. The + button on Home lets you log food outside scheduled slots — it still appears in your gallery and history.
